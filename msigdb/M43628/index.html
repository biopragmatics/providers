SATOH_COLORECTAL_CANCER_MYC_DN

Our transcriptome analyses of paired normal and tumor tissues from 41 patients with colorectal cancer showed that metabolic reprogramming of colorectal cancer is caused chiefly by aberrant MYC expression, which induced at least 215 metabolic reactions by changing the expression levels of metabolic genes and transporter genes. Further, MYC negatively regulated the expression of genes involved in mitochondrial biogenesis and maintenance but positively regulated genes involved in DNA and histone methylation. Knockdown of MYC in colorectal cancer cells reset the altered metabolism and suppressed cell growth.
