Term Nasopharyngeal goblet cell

Cell of the respiratory pseudostratified epithelium. Goblet cells are interspersed between the ciliated cells. They are filled with mucus and therefore appear pale and have a nucleus located in the basal part of the cell. The mucus is secreted to the nasopharyngeal cavity. The mucus is important for maintaining a suitable environment for ciliary function and protection against airborne infectious agents and solid particles.
