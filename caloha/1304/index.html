Term Gall bladder epithelium

Epithelium lining the gall bladder. Tall simple columnar absorptive epithelium. Has characteristics that distinguish it from the absorptive epithelium of other organs such as the intestines. Only tall columnar cells are present in the epithelial layer. The nuclei are in the basal portion of the cell. The cells possess a thin apical striated border. The epithelial cells frequently exhibit distended intercellular spaces at their basal aspect.
