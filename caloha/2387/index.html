Term Sensory neuron

Sensory neurons convey impulses from receptors to the CNS. Processes of these neurons are included in somatic afferent and visceral afferent nerve fibers. Somatic afferent fibers convey sensations of pain, temperature, touch, and pressure from the body surface. In addition, theses fibers convey pain and proprioception (nonconscious sensation) from organs within the body (e.g., muscles, tendons, and joints) to provide the brain with information related to the orientation of the body and limbs. Visceral afferent fibers transmit impulses of pain and other sensations from mucous membranes, glands, and blood vessels.
